# Further Reading

This list provides additional resources for readers interested in exploring topics related to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics in greater depth.

## Foundational Texts

1. **Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016).** *Springer Handbook of Robotics* (2nd ed.). Springer.
   - A comprehensive reference covering all aspects of robotics research and development.

2. **Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2006).** *Robot Modeling and Control*. John Wiley & Sons.
   - Classic textbook covering fundamental principles of robot modeling, planning, and control.

3. **Craig, J. J. (2005).** *Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control* (3rd ed.). Pearson Prentice Hall.
   - An accessible introduction to robotics with emphasis on mechanical and control aspects.

4. **Latombe, J. C. (2012).** *Robot Motion Planning*. Springer Science & Business Media.
   - In-depth treatment of path planning algorithms for robots.

## Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence

5. **Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006).** *How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence*. MIT Press.
   - Explores how the physical body influences cognitive processes and intelligence.

6. **Brooks, R. (1991).** *Intelligence without representation*. Artificial Intelligence, 47(1-3), 139-159.
   - Foundational paper on embodied cognition and subsumption architecture.

7. **Pfeifer, R., & Scheier, C. (1999).** *Understanding Intelligence*. MIT Press.
   - Comprehensive exploration of embodied approaches to artificial intelligence.

## Humanoid Robotics

8. **Ames, A. D. (2014).** *Human-in-the-loop robotics: an introduction*. Morgan & Claypool Publishers.
   - Focuses on human-robot interaction and collaboration.

9. **Khatib, O., Park, H. J., Park, I. W., Bae, J. H., Lee, S., Kim, D., ... & You, B. J. (2009).** *Mechatronic design of KTX-2 and its experimental evaluation for humanoid motion analysis*. IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation.
   - Case study of humanoid robot design and implementation.

10. **Ott, C., Kugi, A., & Hashimoto, K. (2014).** *A whole-body control framework for humanoids operating in human environments*. Advanced Robotics, 28(2), 73-86.
    - Advanced control techniques for humanoid robots operating in human environments.

## Reinforcement Learning and Robotics

11. **Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018).** *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.
    - The definitive textbook on reinforcement learning, essential for understanding robot learning.

12. **Deisenroth, M. P., Fox, D., & Rovatsos, M. (2009).** *Gaussian processes for data-efficient learning in robotics and control*. IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation.
    - Shows how Gaussian processes can be used for efficient learning in robotics.

13. **Kober, J., Bagnell, J. A., & Peters, J. (2013).** *Reinforcement learning in robotics: A survey*. The International Journal of Robotics Research, 32(11), 1238-1274.
    - Comprehensive survey of reinforcement learning applications in robotics.

## Perception and Computer Vision

14. **Szeliski, R. (2022).** *Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications* (2nd ed.). Springer.
    - Comprehensive coverage of computer vision algorithms and their applications.

15. **Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005).** *Probabilistic Robotics*. MIT Press.
    - Covers probabilistic approaches to perception and navigation for robots.

16. **Murphy, K. P. (2023).** *Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.
    - Provides the theoretical foundation for probabilistic approaches in robotics.

## Safety and Ethics

17. **Lin, P., Abney, K., & Bekey, G. A. (Eds.). (2011).** *Robot ethics: The ethical and social implications of robotics*. MIT Press.
    - Explores the ethical dimensions of robotics technology.

18. **Sharkey, A., & Sharkey, N. (2010).** *The-assurance-of-robot-safety: A survey of verification, validation and testing techniques*. Knowledge Engineering Review, 25(3), 305-326.
    - Survey of techniques for ensuring robot safety.

19. **MatariÄ‡, M. J. (2007).** *The robotics primer*. MIT Press.
    - Includes discussion of ethical and societal implications of robotics.

## Simulation and Development Tools

20. **Coumans, E., & Bai, Y. (2016).** *PyBullet, a Python module for physics simulation for games, robotics and machine learning*. https://pybullet.org
    - Documentation and resources for the PyBullet physics engine.

21. **Todorov, E., Erez, T., & Tassa, Y. (2012).** *Mujoco: A physics engine for contact dynamics*. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.
    - Introduction to the Mujoco physics engine.

22. **Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Leibs, J., ... & Ng, A. Y. (2009).** *ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System*. ICRA Workshop on Open Source Software, 3(3.2), 5.
    - Original paper on the Robot Operating System.

## Human-Robot Interaction

23. **Mutlu, B., & Forlizzi, J. (2008).** *A narrative framework for designing social robots*. ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction.
    - Framework for designing social aspects of robots.

24. **Breazeal, C. (2002).** *Designing Sociable Robots*. MIT Press.
    - Pioneering work on social robotics and human-robot interaction.

25. **Goodrich, M. A., & Schultz, A. C. (2007).** *Human-robot interaction: a survey*. Foundations and Trends in Human-Computer Interaction, 1(3), 203-275.
    - Comprehensive survey of human-robot interaction research.

## Multi-Modal Perception

26. **Thruns, S. (2002).** *Probabilistic approaches to multi-robot perception*. Multi-robot systems: From swarms to intelligent automata, 1, 79-95.
    - Approaches to perception in multi-robot systems.

27. **Dragone, M., O'Hare, G. M. P., & O'Grady, R. (2011).** *Resourceful robot ecosystems: A distributed intelligence approach to robotic cognition*. Adaptive Behavior, 19(5), 348-365.
    - On distributed intelligence and perception in robotic systems.

## Dynamics and Control

28. **Featherstone, R. (2008).** *Rigid Body Dynamics Algorithms*. Springer.
    - Essential algorithms for computing robot dynamics.

29. **Slotine, J. J. E., & Li, W. (1991).** *Applied Nonlinear Control*. Prentice Hall.
    - Nonlinear control approaches relevant to robotics.

30. **Murray, R. M., Li, Z., & Sastry, S. S. (1994).** *A Mathematical Introduction to Robotic Manipulation*. CRC Press.
    - Mathematical foundations of robotic manipulation.
